Instructions for use of this factorizer:

Return to the Math APIs page.

  • general:
    • After ...herokuapp.com above you should type /json/ followed by your particular input. Spaces are allowed - but discouraged - because %20 will replace each space after you hit return, thereby making the address uglier. E-notation is not allowed. All numbers must be natural numbers less than 263 (which approximately equals "9" followed by 18 zeros).
    • If you want the response to be html rather than json, simply omit /json from the address.
  • greatest common divisor (GCD):
    • Your input should be a bracket-enclosed list of one or more comma-separated numbers.
    • examples:
      inputresult
      [9,12]3
      [9,10]1
      [13]13
      [8,10,12]2
  • prime factorization of a real integer:
    • Your input should simply be a number.
    • The factors property of the json output will be an array of 2-element subarrays. The zeroth element is a prime factor, and the other is the power to which it is raised when forming the factorization.
    • examples:
      inputresultjson
      359prime
      36023·32·5[[2,3],[3,2],[5,1]]
  • factorization of (complex) Gaussian integer:
    • Between ...herokuapp.com and /json/ you should include /complex.
    • Restrict your Gaussian integer to be in the first quadrant of the complex plane, including the positive real axis, but not including the positive imaginary axis. Accordingly, the complex number should be of the form m + ni, where m is a natural number and n is a whole number (ie, may be 0).
